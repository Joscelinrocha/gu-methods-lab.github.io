Georgetown Methods Lab

Methods Lab is a trainee-run laboratory established for the purpose of providing a space beyond the classroom to exchange ideas, share code, receive feedback, learn modern research methods, and foster a culture of open science in empirical psychology and cognitive neuroscience at Georgetown University.

Social: Null Results Blitz
February 19, 2019

Trainees will prepare a quick 5 minute presentation on a null result, followed by a social event with catering by Chipotle. The purpose of this event is to offer an outlet for experiments that do not reach traditional significance levels and discuss the implications associated with biases against work that did not reject the null hypothesis. Find us in MedDent NE403.

Methods Lab: Intro to GitHub
April 11, 2019

We will first introduce GitHub as an effective collaboration tool. We will then hold a "lab" session, during which we will break up into smaller groups to collectively contribute to the GU MethLab GitHub repository by creating and sharing helpful scripts/resources/tutorials for the Georgetown Cognitive Neuroscience community and beyond.
